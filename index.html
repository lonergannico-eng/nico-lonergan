About

Researching how Louisiana wetlands respond to restoration and environmental stress.

I am a master’s student in Oceanography & Coastal Sciences at Louisiana State University and a graduate researcher in the Wetland and Aquatic Biogeochemistry Laboratory. My work combines field sampling, laboratory biogeochemistry, and remote sensing to understand how coastal wetlands respond to sediment restoration, salinity stress, drought, and storm surge.

My current projects focus on the biogeochemical impacts of thin-layer placement in Louisiana’s emerging mangrove systems and on using salinity records with satellite-based indicators to compare drought-driven and storm-driven stress across coastal wetlands.

Research

Current research themes

Thin-layer placement & mangrove biogeochemistry

Field and laboratory work examining how dredged sediment placement influences nutrient cycling, redox conditions, and ecosystem processes in Louisiana’s emerging mangrove wetlands.

Drought, storm surge & wetland health

Remote sensing and long-term salinity analyses focused on how droughts and storm surges intensify saltwater intrusion and how those stressors appear in wetland vegetation patterns.

Nutrient cycling in coastal restoration systems

Biogeochemical studies of marsh and mangrove soils, including nutrient fluxes, phosphorus dynamics, and restoration-relevant changes in soil and water chemistry.
